# How To Build Trust Through Feedback & Support

Trust is vital to building positive school culture, developing strong teacher-admin relationships, and creating feedback cycles that generate real growth. But how do we build trust? It's important to remember that trust is a two-way street. To trust your teachers is to value them and believe in them; to have your teachers' trust is to provide them a confidant and a collaborator to help them along their journey of growth.



You can build trust through Bullseye Feedback & Support using the following methods:



**1. Conduct Walkthroughs Using Strengths-Based Feedback**

We recommend taking a strengths-based approach where you identify all the things a teacher is doing well while only providing one or two areas for growth. This may mean leaving some objectives blank within a Bullseye session if they aren’t the highest leverage action step. This will allow for more focused, manageable, and actionable feedback for teachers.



**2. Create Opportunities for Reflection & Collaboration**

When delivering feedback & scores, it's important to provide context so that teachers have a better understanding as to why they received the feedback/ score they did. But to ensure teacher point of view is included within feedback cycles, we recommend asking questions to encourage teacher reflection. Not only does this allow for the teachers to feel heard and more in ownership of their growth, but it also offers opportunities for the teacher and admin to collaborate on teachers' growth.



**3. Support The Teacher AND The Person**

Teachers are going above and beyond every day for their students. It's important that we are providing them the support they need as teachers and also as individuals. Trust is not built between teachers and administrators; rather trust is built between two people. Personalizing support to ensure teachers are feeling heard, valued, and respected as individuals is key to building trust.





**HELPFUL HINTS FROM BULLSEYE USERS:**



At Le Grand High School, the administrative team has prioritized building a positive adult culture around observation and feedback using Bullseye. Teachers appreciate that they are receiving feedback and use it as an opportunity to collaborate with their administrators. To build this rapport, Principal Javier Martinez has focused on the following steps:

1. Make sure feedback is positive and focus on questions such as “I wonder…?”

2. Ask questions about what is happening in the lesson which encourages teachers to respond to feedback and engage in dialogue

3. Follow up after the observation for a debrief conversation especially if the teacher is struggling and ask questions such as “Did I miss something?” or “What can I do to support you?”
