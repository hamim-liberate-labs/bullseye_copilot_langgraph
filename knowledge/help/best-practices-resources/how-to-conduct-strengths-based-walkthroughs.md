# How to Conduct Strengths-Based Walkthroughs

When conducting a walkthrough, it may be tempting to capture everything you see in the lesson. However, receiving feedback with several yellow/orange scores or areas to improve can feel overwhelming and demoralizing for teachers. It's important to remember that feedback is best received in manageable, bite-size pieces.



Check out the steps below to see how feedback can be delivered in an empowering and actionable way for teachers to easily and confidently implement next steps, and feel successful doing so.



**Conducting Strengths-Based Walkthroughs:**

1. In the session notes, capture what you observe without bias or judgment. This helps to provide context to the lesson. Upload media as needed.



2. Identify all of the strengths from the lesson. Add YES / GREEN scores to indicate what the teacher is doing well (research shows this is the best way to improve practice). Be sure to include objective notes so the teacher knows exactly what went well.



3. Identify one or at most two areas of growth, adding a score and an objective note to provide context. Asking questions is a great way to encourage teacher reflection and create opportunities for collaboration.



4. If needed, schedule a check-in with a teacher to debrief the lesson and see how you can better support them.
