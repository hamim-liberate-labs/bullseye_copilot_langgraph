# How To Plan Staff-Wide Professional Development

There are several ways that Bullseye data can be leveraged to inform professional development. Once you have identified an objective on which your staff would benefit from receiving additional professional development, it is time to begin planning. Below are a few options for how to get started with planning:



1. Plan a professional development that shows what the objective entails and what it looks like to be effective at that particular skill. Provide opportunities for practice and/or reflection on how to apply the skill to their classroom.



2. Showcase examples of excellence in your own school. You can click into each objective to see which teachers are already demonstrating mastery in that area. Ask these teachers if you can share examples of their success. This is a great way to allow teachers to learn from their peers.



3. Identify the teachers who are really effective at a certain skill and ask them to lead a short portion of professional development. Ask the teacher to speak about what they have done to excel at this skill and leave time for questions from the rest of the staff. This is a great opportunity to provide leadership opportunities for your staff and leverage what is happening in your school.
