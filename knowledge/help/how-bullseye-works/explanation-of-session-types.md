# Explanation of Session Types

Bullseye offers multiple ways to structure and collect instructional data based on your goals. Watch the video below, or keep reading to learn about each session type.

[https://player.vimeo.com/video/1156633377](https://player.vimeo.com/video/1156633377)

# Types of Sessions in Bullseye:

## **1. Narrative Sessions**

- Capture open-ended notes and evidence aligned to your instructional framework

- Commonly used for coaching and non-evaluative classroom visits

- Support collaborative, reflective conversations between coaches and teachers

## **2. Mastery Sessions**

- Capture notes and evidence and assign Mastery Scores

- Use structured scoring scales (e.g., 3-, 4-, or 5-point scales)

- Often used for more structured or evaluative observations

- Helpful for measuring performance or progress against defined criteria

## **3. Checklists**

- Can be used within Narrative or Mastery Sessions

- Allow observers to quickly mark what was observed or not observed

- Designed for efficient, high-level data collection

## **4. Self-Reflection Sessions**

- Completed by a user on their own practice

- The user is automatically set as the learner

- Used to:

  - Build familiarity with the instructional framework

  - Encourage reflection and professional dialogue

  - Identify strengths and areas for growth

  - Generate trend data over time

Self-Reflections empower educators to take ownership of their development while providing valuable insights at both the individual and school-wide level.
