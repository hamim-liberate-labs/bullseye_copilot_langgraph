# How do Teachers Respond to Feedback?

An important part of effective walkthroughs is the dialogue that feedback creates.

Bullseye offers two ways to communicate: **comments** and **email**.

**Comments**

You and your team can add comments within a session. Whether it is a teacher who wants to reply to feedback or an administrator looking to add an extra note to a session, commenting enables better communication and collaboration.

1. Click into a session

2. Click "Comments"

3. Select the names of people you want to notify

4. Add your comment

5. Click "Add Comment"

**Email**

When a teacher receives an email notifying them that their session is ready, the email will come from the observer. Teachers can respond to you by replying directly to the email they receive.

Share the description below with your staff!

---

Administrators leverage Bullseye to help be better instructional leaders for their staff. They capture classroom observations to provide feedback and better understand the needs of their staff to provide resources and support.

After an administrator completes an observation, they will notify you that the session is ready to be reviewed in Bullseye. You will receive the email from the person who conducted the observation.

You can simply reply to the email to respond to the feedback you’ve received or you can **add a comment** on the session. Tag the person you want to be notified about the comment.
